About Heart Disease

Understanding cardiovascular disease — the world's leading cause of death and why data analysis matters.

The Problem

Why cardiovascular health matters

Heart disease encompasses a group of conditions affecting the structure and function of the heart — from coronary artery disease to arrhythmias and congenital defects. It remains the number one killer globally, responsible for 17.9 million deaths each year.

Research from the National Heart, Lung and Blood Institute identifies obesity, sedentary lifestyle and smoking as the three most critical modifiable risk factors. This project analyses those patterns using real CDC survey data.

Our dataset — derived from the CDC's BRFSS 2020 survey — contains 319,795 records and 18 variables covering demographics, lifestyle habits, and pre-existing conditions.

Risk Factors

What drives cardiovascular risk?


🍔

Obesity

Excess body weight increases strain on the heart, raises blood pressure, and promotes plaque buildup in arteries.

🛋️

Sedentary Lifestyle

Physical inactivity weakens the cardiovascular system and is strongly correlated with heart disease incidence.

🚬

Smoking

Tobacco use damages blood vessel walls, reduces oxygen delivery and roughly doubles the risk of heart disease.

🍷

Alcohol Consumption

Heavy drinking raises blood pressure, weakens the heart muscle and contributes to arrhythmia and heart failure.

🩺

Diabetes

High blood glucose damages blood vessels and nerves controlling the heart — doubling cardiovascular risk.

😰

Chronic Stress

Long-term stress triggers hormonal responses that raise blood pressure and promote inflammatory pathways.
